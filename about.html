Why We Built This

Built for the people who couldn't afford to get it wrong.

Yes Car No Car exists for two people. The one about to spend their savings on a car they can't afford to get wrong. And the one already driving a car with no idea what's coming.

The Moment

Picture a single mom shopping for a minivan on a Saturday. She needs it to work. She needs it to not fail her on the way to school, on the way to work, on the way to the emergency room at 2am. She has done her research. She has read every review. She is standing in a parking lot about to spend money she has carefully saved — and every single person in that transaction knows more about that vehicle than she does. The seller knows what's wrong with it. The dealer knows what it's worth. The mechanic she'd want in her corner isn't there. She's making the most important financial decision of her month with the least information in the room.

Now picture someone who already owns a car. It runs fine. No warning lights. No strange noises. Nothing to suggest anything is wrong — because nothing feels wrong. What they don't know is that a specific component on their specific engine, at their specific mileage, is the one thing standing between them and a $3,000 repair. The information exists. It lives in service patterns and documented failure histories. It just doesn't live anywhere a normal person looks on a Tuesday.

Most people hear "vehicle report" and think about the moment of purchase. That moment matters — we built for it. But there are 290 million registered vehicles in America and only a fraction change hands each year. The biggest audience for what we do isn't people buying cars. It's people who already own one — and want to know what's coming before it costs them $3,000. No warning light. No noise. Just a car that was fine yesterday and isn't today, because nobody told them what to watch for on this exact engine, at this exact mileage, in plain English. That's the other half of what we built.

"That's not a knowledge gap. That's a design."

The used-car transaction is set up so the information flows one way, and it's not yours. And the car you already own doesn't come with a manual that tells you what to watch for on this exact engine, at this exact mileage, in plain English. We looked at both of those things and got angry — because the information existed. Nobody was putting it on the right side of the table. So we did.

The Decision

The information to fix this already existed. Service histories, common failure points for specific models, recall records, real market pricing — it was all out there, scattered across places most buyers never think to look and wouldn't know how to read if they did. The problem was never that the information didn't exist. The problem was that it never sat on the buyer's side of the table.

And it never would, on its own. Nobody who makes money when you buy a car has a reason to hand you a clear-eyed verdict before you sign. Not the dealer. Not the platform selling you leads. Everyone's incentives point the same direction, and it isn't yours. So we stopped waiting for someone with a financial stake in the sale to do it, and built the thing ourselves.

That independence isn't a selling point we added later. It's the reason this exists at all. No dealer money. No advertiser money. No industry partnerships, no data-sharing deals, no quiet arrangements with anyone on the other side of the table. The verdict you get comes from the car's actual record — not from who's writing the check.

For the People

This isn't a venture-backed company trying to capture a market before the next funding round. It's a team that watched friends, family, and total strangers get outmaneuvered on the biggest purchase they'd make that year — and decided that was one too many. Yes Car No Car exists because buyers deserve someone in their corner who isn't also getting paid by the seller.

That's why the first 500 people on the waitlist get their first report at half price. Not a growth trick, not a countdown timer designed to rush you. It's simple: if you showed up early and believed this was worth building before it existed, that should mean something real. This stays small and independent on purpose — because small and independent is what makes it impossible to buy.

The Promise

This is what we commit to. Not as a marketing position — as the permanent foundation of the business.

The verdict is always free. Before you spend anything, you get the answer.

One email at launch. No spam. Sign up and that's what you get — one message when we go live, nothing before or after it.

No dealer relationships. Not now, not later, not ever.

No advertiser deals. Nobody pays us to influence the verdict.

No conflicts of interest. The product makes money when buyers pay for intelligence. Full stop.

Independent. Built for everyone who drives. Launching soon.
