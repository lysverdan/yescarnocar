The Full Report

Everything about that car.
Nothing held back.

The full report is an editorial dossier on that exact VIN — not a data dump. Verified history, the intelligence to read it, and a verdict. Here's what's in it.

01

Verified Service History

Every recorded service visit — date, mileage, shop name, and exactly what was done. Not self-reported. Verified. You can see whether the car was dealer-maintained or bounced between shops, whether the serpentine belt was replaced last month, whether the brakes were done at 53,000 miles.

This is the record the seller hopes you never ask for.

Date · Location · Shop · Work performed
02

Accident & Damage Records

Verified accident history — not just whether something was reported, but what was damaged and what the record shows. Title brands, structural damage flags, fire, flood, hail, salvage, lemon, and manufacturer buyback — all checked and reported plainly.

A clean title doesn't mean a clean history. We show you the difference.

Accident records · Title brands · Damage verification
03

Full Recall Detail

Not a count. The full text of every recall on this vehicle — the system affected, what can go wrong, the corrective action, and whether it was completed. Some recalls are minor. Some tell you to stop driving the car immediately.

You deserve to know which one you're looking at.

Full recall text · System · Corrective action · Status
04

Ownership & Mileage Timeline

How many owners, how long each held it, where the car was registered, and whether it was used personally, as a fleet vehicle, rental, taxi, or commercially. Paired with a full odometer timeline — every recorded mileage reading from delivery to today — so rollbacks and usage gaps are visible at a glance.

Ownership history · Usage verification · Odometer snapshots
05

Market Value — 12 Data Points

What the car is actually worth — trade-in, private party, and dealer retail, each broken out across four condition tiers. Twelve reference points so you understand exactly where the asking price sits relative to the market.

Trade-in · Private party · Dealer retail · Four condition tiers
06

Documented Failure Patterns

Not generic reliability ratings. The specific, expensive, model-particular problems that sink buyers. We know what this exact engine, in this exact year, tends to do — the Hyundai Theta II, the Equinox oil vacuum pump, the 948TE transmission. We tell you before you sign anything.

Model · Year · Engine · Known failure modes
07

Real Repair Costs

Dollar figures, not vague warnings. If the timing chain on this generation is known to go at 90,000 miles and you're at 82,000, we tell you what that repair costs and what it costs to ignore it. Catching one expensive repair before it happens pays for the product many times over.

Parts · Labour · Cost to ignore
08

Tailored Mechanic Checklist

A pre-purchase inspection is only as good as the instructions you give the mechanic. Yes Car No Car generates a checklist tailored to your exact VIN — the specific things to inspect on this car, at this mileage, with this history. You walk in already knowing what matters.

VIN-specific · Ready to hand to your mechanic
09

A Negotiating Angle

The report ends with what to do with everything in it. If there's a documented issue, we give you the language to take back to the seller — how to frame the ask, what number is reasonable, and when to walk instead of negotiate.

Specific to this car's findings
10

Five Follow-Up Questions

The report answers what we know. Five follow-up questions come with every report, tied to that VIN, for as long as you own the decision. Ask about a specific recall. Ask whether the seller's repair quote is reasonable. The conversation stays open.

Tied to your VIN · No expiry
