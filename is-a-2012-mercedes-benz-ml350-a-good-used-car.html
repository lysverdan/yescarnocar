Vehicle Intelligence · 2012 Mercedes-Benz ML350 4MATIC

Is a 2012 Mercedes-Benz ML350 a Good Used Car to Buy?

Yes — with conditions. The third-generation ML fixed the engine flaw that haunted the previous one, but a clean cold start and a proper Mercedes-level scan are what separate a good one from a project.

GOOD CAR — WITH CONDITIONS

Why it's a good car

The 2012 ML350 is the third-generation M-Class — the W166, a clean-sheet redesign for 2012. Mercedes used the launch to retire the troubled M272 V6, whose balance-shaft gear defect turned a lot of earlier MLs into expensive problems, and bring in the all-new direct-injected M276 3.5-litre V6 (around 302 hp). That single change matters: the specific failure that defined the previous generation is gone. It is paired with the 7G-Tronic Plus seven-speed automatic and full-time 4MATIC all-wheel drive, and it rides on steel coil springs as standard, with AIRMATIC air suspension only as an option.

Does it have a timing belt or a timing chain?

A timing chain. The M276 V6 is chain-driven, so there is no scheduled timing-belt job. The thing to listen for instead is a brief rattle from the camshaft-adjuster and timing-chain area on a cold start — a known M276 concern that is tied closely to oil-change discipline. A clean, quiet cold start is exactly what you want.

The things to check before you buy

  • Cold-start rattle Start it stone cold and listen at the front of the engine for the first several seconds. A quick settle is normal; a lingering metallic rattle points to the cam adjuster or timing chain, and that is the expensive end of this engine.
  • Transmission behaviour The seven-speed is reliable when its fluid has actually been serviced — ignore the sealed-for-life marketing. You want clean, prompt shifts with no slip or flare.
  • Air conditioning The A/C compressor is a common failure on hot-climate examples; confirm it blows genuinely cold.
  • Suspension type Confirm whether it is on standard steel coils (simple and cheap) or the optional AIRMATIC air suspension (expensive) by looking for a coil spring versus a rubber air bellows at the corners.
  • Intake-manifold tumble-flap motor A documented M276 item that usually throws a check-engine light before it strands anyone.
  • Proper diagnostics These need a Mercedes-capable scan tool — not just a generic code reader — to read the transmission, SRS/airbag, and air-suspension modules.
  • Open recalls Confirm any outstanding Mercedes recalls were completed; the dealer performs them free.

What would make you walk

  • A rattle that won't clear A loud, sustained cold-start rattle, or any chain-area knock under acceleration.
  • Coolant in the oil Milky, cream-coloured residue under the oil cap.
  • A tired transmission Slip, flare, or harsh engagement despite a recent fluid service.
  • An SRS fault An airbag or seatbelt warning light that will not clear.
